About Rice

Rice at the heart of Ahero's irrigation landscape

Rice is a staple cereal that powers Kenyan diets and creates livelihoods. Ahero uses modern irrigation, local expertise and integrated production steps to grow rice varieties suited for the climate and the market.

What is rice?

Rice is an annual cereal crop grown for its edible grain. It is the second most consumed food crop in the world and serves as a staple for billions of people.

Importance of rice

Rice provides energy, income, and resilience for farming communities. In Ahero, rice cultivation supports food security, employment and rural development.

Rice farming in Ahero

Ahero Irrigation Scheme uses water management and improved practices to raise reliable rice yields. Farmers use certified seed, managed nurseries, and field maintenance throughout the season.

Nutritional value

Rice supplies carbohydrates, a small amount of protein, and essential minerals. In Kenya, rice is a central energy source and an important ingredient in many traditional dishes.

Value Addition

Turning rice into products

The Ahero scheme supports rice processing and value addition to increase incomes and diversify use. Rice byproducts become nutritious foods, livestock feed and sustainable fuel.

Rice flour

Processed rice grains turn into flour for bread, porridge and snacks. It adds nutrition and expands the market for rice byproducts.

Rice cakes

Rice cakes are a value-added snack made from puffed or pressed rice. They increase consumer demand and help reduce post-harvest losses.

Rice porridge

Rice porridge is a traditional dish rich in energy and easy to digest. It is made from boiled rice and often enriched with milk or vegetables.

Rice bran feed

Bran from milling is a nutrient-rich livestock feed. It supports dairy and poultry production in communities around Ahero.

Rice husk briquettes

Rice husks can be compacted into briquettes and used as a renewable fuel. They reduce waste and lower the need for firewood.

Benefits

Why rice farming matters

Rice production supports Ahero's economic and social goals. It creates jobs, strengthens food systems, and contributes to regional industry.

Income source

Rice sales create income for farmers and their families throughout the crop cycle.

Food security

Rice is a reliable staple crop that helps stabilize food supplies for local communities.

Employment

Growing rice generates labor for nursery work, planting, maintenance and harvest.

Industrial use

Rice becomes raw material for milling, flour production, animal feed and briquette manufacturing.
